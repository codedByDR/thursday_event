2. Executive summary

This document defines the requirements for an AI-assisted flexible work and life balance request system that enables employees to request arrangements such as work from home, shift changes, and caregiving support. The system helps employees create clear, policy-aligned requests, routes them for approval, tracks status, and monitors outcomes over time to promote fairness and consistent decision-making across the organization.

3. Goals and success metrics

Goals

  • Enable employees to submit flexible work requests quickly and clearly.
  • Improve request quality via AI guidance and professionally formatted justifications.
  • Ensure routing, approvals, and escalations follow defined policy and hierarchy rules.
  • Increase consistency and fairness across teams through standardized criteria, monitoring, and reporting.
  • Create an auditable, compliant record of requests, decisions, and outcomes.

Success metrics

  • Median time to submit a complete request (minutes).
  • Approval cycle time by request type (hours or days).
  • Percentage of requests requiring rework due to missing information.
  • Employee satisfaction score for the request experience.
  • Consistency indicators (variance in approval rates across teams after controlling for role and location).
  • Percentage of decisions with complete policy tags and audit logs.

4. Scope

In scope

  • Guided request intake for flexible work arrangements.
  • AI assistance for structuring requests and generating justifications.
  • Automatic routing to approvers based on hierarchy and request type.
  • Policy tagging (policy identifiers and decision criteria references).
  • Status tracking, reminders, and escalation.
  • Outcome monitoring, feedback loops, and re-evaluation for ongoing arrangements.
  • Reporting dashboards for fairness and operational performance.

Out of scope

  • Payroll changes and benefits enrollment workflows (unless explicitly integrated later).
  • Full case management for employee relations disputes beyond an appeal step.
  • Scheduling and workforce management execution (the system records decisions; execution may be external).

Assumptions

  • Organization has defined flexible work policies (or will define them) with identifiers.
  • There is a reliable source of org hierarchy (HRIS or directory).
  • Users authenticate via SSO or another identity mechanism.

Constraints

  • Must support privacy and sensitive personal context.
  • Must avoid biased AI outputs and protect sensitive attributes.
  • Must retain auditability for compliance and internal review.

5. Personas and roles

Employee (Requester)

  • Description: Employee submitting a flexible work or caregiving support request.
  • Goals: Submit a clear request, understand status, receive a timely decision, and track outcomes.
  • Key permissions: Create and edit own requests (until submitted), view status, view decision rationale, provide feedback, request reconsideration within allowed rules.

Manager (Approver)

  • Description: Direct manager or delegated manager approving requests.
  • Goals: Make consistent decisions, understand policy guidance, assess impact on team operations.
  • Key permissions: View requests for direct reports, approve/deny, request more info, add conditions, view policy references, view history and prior similar decisions (where allowed).

HR Business Partner / HR Reviewer (Approver or Co-approver)

  • Description: Ensures policy compliance and fairness.
  • Goals: Ensure decisions align with policy, reduce risk, maintain consistency across teams.
  • Key permissions: Review requests, co-approve, override or escalate (as allowed), view fairness dashboards, access audit logs.

Compliance / Legal / Security Reviewer (Conditional approver)

  • Description: Reviews requests that trigger special conditions (country rules, data access, security).
  • Goals: Ensure compliance with legal/security constraints.
  • Key permissions: Review only flagged requests, comment, approve/deny within scope, add required controls.

HR Operations Admin (Admin)

  • Description: Configures policies, workflows, categories, and reporting.
  • Goals: Keep the system accurate, maintain templates and routing, manage retention settings.
  • Key permissions: Manage policy library, configure routing rules, manage categories, manage SLA rules, manage integrations, manage reporting configurations.

6. User journeys / key workflows

Workflow A: Submit a flexible work request (guided intake)

  • Trigger: Employee selects “New request”.
  • Preconditions: Employee authenticated; role and manager known.
  • Main flow
    1. Employee selects request type (or “not sure”).
    2. System prompts for arrangement details (duration, dates, schedule, location, urgency).
    3. Employee enters free-text context.
    4. AI suggests missing fields and clarifying questions.
    5. AI drafts a structured request summary and justification.
    6. Employee reviews, edits, and confirms.
    7. System validates required fields and policy constraints.
    8. Employee submits.
  • Alternate flows
    • Employee saves as draft and returns later.
    • Employee chooses “No AI help” and submits manually (still validated).
  • Postconditions: Request created, assigned a tracking ID, routed for approvals.

Workflow B: Approval and routing

  • Trigger: Request submitted.
  • Preconditions: Routing rules configured; approver availability known.
  • Main flow
    1. System determines route: manager only, HR only, or manager + HR (and conditional reviewers).
    2. Approvers are notified.
    3. Approver reviews request, policy tags, and AI-generated summary.
    4. Approver approves, denies, or requests more information.
    5. System updates status and notifies employee.
  • Alternate flows
    • Missing approver: route to delegate or HR ops queue.
    • SLA breach: auto-escalate to next-level manager or HR reviewer.
  • Postconditions: Decision recorded, audit log updated, outcome monitoring plan started if approved.

Workflow C: Request changes, withdrawal, and reconsideration

  • Trigger: Employee edits before decision, withdraws, or appeals after denial.
  • Preconditions: Rules allow edits or appeal within time window.
  • Main flow
    1. Employee requests reconsideration with additional information.
    2. System routes appeal per policy.
    3. Decision updated with rationale and audit record.
  • Postconditions: Final decision stored; reporting updated.

Workflow D: Outcome monitoring and feedback

  • Trigger: Request approved (or denied) and monitoring schedule begins.
  • Preconditions: Monitoring cadence configured.
  • Main flow
    1. System schedules check-ins (for example, 30/60/90 days).
    2. Employee and manager provide feedback on effectiveness.
    3. AI summarizes feedback trends and flags risks (burnout, productivity concerns) without making sensitive inferences.
    4. System recommends re-evaluation or adjustments.
  • Postconditions: Outcome records saved and available for analytics.

7. Functional requirements

Module: Request intake

  • FR-1: Create request drafts
    • Description: Employees can create, save, and edit flexible work requests as drafts.
    • Priority: Must
    • Acceptance criteria:
      • Given an authenticated employee, when they start a request, then a draft is created with status “Draft”.
    • Data captured: Request type, dates, schedule needs, urgency, free text, attachments metadata (if allowed).
    • Errors/validations: Required fields enforced on submit.
  • FR-2: Guided data collection
    • Description: System guides employees through required and optional fields based on request type and region.
    • Priority: Must
    • Acceptance criteria:
      • Given a selected request type, when the employee proceeds, then the form shows type-specific required fields.
    • Data captured: Type-specific details.
    • Errors/validations: Missing required fields block submission.

Module: AI assistance (guidance + justification)

  • FR-3: AI clarification prompts
    • Description: AI suggests clarifying questions to improve completeness and professionalism.
    • Priority: Should
    • Acceptance criteria:
      • Given incomplete free text, when AI assist is enabled, then the system suggests specific missing details (dates, duration, constraints).
    • Data captured: Prompt responses, AI suggestion log.
    • Errors/validations: AI suggestions must not force disclosure of sensitive attributes.
  • FR-4: AI-generated justification formatting
    • Description: AI converts informal reasoning into a structured justification aligned to company norms.
    • Priority: Must
    • Acceptance criteria:
      • Given employee free text, when “Generate justification” is clicked, then the output includes summary, requested arrangement, business impact mitigation, and timing.
    • Data captured: Original text, generated text, edit history.
    • Errors/validations: Toxic or policy-violating content flagged and must be corrected before submission.
  • FR-5: Policy-aware suggestions (with placeholders if unknown)
    • Description: AI suggests applicable request categories and policy references using internal policy identifiers.
    • Priority: Should
    • Acceptance criteria:
      • Given request details, when AI analyzes, then it suggests policy tags like POLICY-FLEX-### (placeholder if unknown).
    • Data captured: Suggested policy tags, confidence, user acceptance of tags.
    • Errors/validations: If policies are not configured, system uses placeholders and flags admin setup need.

Module: Approval workflow and routing

  • FR-6: Configurable routing rules
    • Description: System routes requests based on hierarchy, request type, region, and thresholds (duration, frequency).
    • Priority: Must
    • Acceptance criteria:
      • Given a submitted request, when routing runs, then the correct approver chain is assigned according to configured rules.
    • Data captured: Routing rule used, assigned approvers, timestamps.
    • Errors/validations: If routing cannot be determined, send to a fallback queue.
  • FR-7: Approver actions and rationale
    • Description: Approvers can approve, deny, request more info, or approve with conditions, with required rationale fields.
    • Priority: Must
    • Acceptance criteria:
      • Given an approver, when denying, then rationale is required and stored.
    • Data captured: Decision, rationale, conditions, timestamps.
    • Errors/validations: Decisions require required fields; invalid status transitions blocked.
  • FR-8: SLA tracking and escalation
    • Description: System tracks approval SLAs and escalates automatically.
    • Priority: Should
    • Acceptance criteria:
      • Given an approval step is overdue, when SLA threshold is exceeded, then an escalation notification is sent and escalation logged.
    • Data captured: SLA targets, escalations, reminders.
    • Errors/validations: Prevent escalation loops.

Module: Policy tagging and compliance

  • FR-9: Mandatory policy tags on decision
    • Description: Each final decision must include one or more policy tags and decision criteria references.
    • Priority: Must
    • Acceptance criteria:
      • Given a request is being decided, when final decision is submitted, then at least one policy tag is present.
    • Data captured: Policy IDs (e.g., POLICY-FLEX-001), criteria codes.
    • Errors/validations: Missing tags block finalization (unless admin override enabled with justification).
  • FR-10: Compliance flags and conditional review
    • Description: Requests triggering compliance conditions (country restrictions, security constraints) require additional review steps.
    • Priority: Must
    • Acceptance criteria:
      • Given a request meets a conditional rule, when routed, then an additional reviewer step is inserted.
    • Data captured: Triggered condition, reviewer notes.
    • Errors/validations: Conditional step cannot be skipped.

Module: Notifications and reminders

  • FR-11: Status notifications
    • Description: System notifies employees and approvers on submission, decision, info requests, escalations, and monitoring check-ins.
    • Priority: Must
    • Acceptance criteria:
      • Given a status change, when it occurs, then notifications are sent via configured channels.
    • Data captured: Notification events, delivery status.
    • Errors/validations: Respect user notification preferences and quiet hours.

Module: Outcome monitoring and feedback

  • FR-12: Monitoring plan creation
    • Description: Approved requests automatically create an outcome monitoring plan with configurable cadence.
    • Priority: Must
    • Acceptance criteria:
      • Given an approved request, when the decision is finalized, then monitoring tasks are scheduled.
    • Data captured: Check-in schedule, feedback prompts.
    • Errors/validations: Monitoring must pause if employment status changes.
  • FR-13: Feedback capture and AI summarization
    • Description: Collect feedback from employees and managers; AI summarizes themes and flags potential issues for review.
    • Priority: Should
    • Acceptance criteria:
      • Given completed feedback, when AI summarizes, then it produces a neutral summary and avoids inferring protected attributes.
    • Data captured: Feedback responses, summaries, flags.
    • Errors/validations: Allow users to opt out of AI summarization where required.

Module: Reporting dashboard

  • FR-14: Operational dashboards
    • Description: Provide dashboards for volume, cycle time, SLA compliance, and outcomes by request type.
    • Priority: Must
    • Acceptance criteria:
      • Given a time range, when a dashboard is viewed, then metrics match underlying request data.
    • Data captured: Aggregations and filters.
    • Errors/validations: Access control enforced for dashboard viewers.
  • FR-15: Fairness and consistency monitoring
    • Description: Provide fairness monitoring views to detect inconsistent decision patterns across teams while handling protected attributes appropriately.
    • Priority: Must
    • Acceptance criteria:
      • Given appropriate permissions, when HR leadership views fairness metrics, then data is aggregated and de-identified where required.
    • Data captured: Aggregated metrics, anomaly flags.
    • Errors/validations: Prevent exposing protected attributes at an individual level unless explicitly permitted by policy and law.

Module: Administration and configuration

  • FR-16: Policy library management
    • Description: Admins can manage policies with identifiers, applicability rules, and text guidance.
    • Priority: Must
    • Acceptance criteria:
      • Given an admin, when they update a policy, then new requests use updated guidance while existing requests retain a versioned reference.
    • Data captured: Policy versions, effective dates.
    • Errors/validations: Prevent deleting policies referenced by requests; allow deprecation.
  • FR-17: Audit log configuration and export
    • Description: Admins can configure retention and export for audit.
    • Priority: Must
    • Acceptance criteria:
      • Given an admin, when an export is generated, then it includes immutable event history for selected requests.
    • Data captured: Export metadata.
    • Errors/validations: Export access restricted and logged.

8. Data model (logical)

Entity: Request

  • Fields: request_id, requester_id, request_type, status, created_at, submitted_at, region, urgency, start_date, end_date, schedule_details, free_text_reason, structured_summary, ai_generated_justification, final_justification, attachments_metadata
  • Relationships: has many ApprovalSteps, has many PolicyTags, has many Feedback entries, has many AuditLog events

Entity: RequestType

  • Fields: type_id, name (WFH, shift change, caregiving support), required_fields_config, default_routing_rule_id

Entity: PolicyReference

  • Fields: policy_id (e.g., POLICY-FLEX-###), title, description, region_applicability, effective_date, version, criteria_codes

Entity: PolicyTag (join)

  • Fields: request_id, policy_id, criteria_code, tag_source (AI/user/admin), confidence, created_at

Entity: ApprovalStep

  • Fields: step_id, request_id, step_order, approver_role, approver_user_id, status, decision, rationale, conditions, due_at, decided_at
  • Relationships: belongs to Request

Entity: DecisionOutcome

  • Fields: request_id, final_decision (approved/denied/withdrawn), decision_date, decision_owner_id, decision_summary

Entity: Feedback

  • Fields: feedback_id, request_id, submitted_by_role, submitted_by_user_id, submitted_at, effectiveness_rating, comments, issues_flagged, ai_summary

Entity: AuditLog

  • Fields: audit_id, request_id, actor_user_id, actor_role, event_type, event_payload, created_at, ip_address (if applicable), source_system

9. Non-functional requirements

  • Security and privacy: Encrypt data in transit and at rest; minimize sensitive fields; support redaction workflows where required.
  • RBAC: Role-based access by requester/manager/HR/compliance/admin; least privilege defaults.
  • Auditability: Immutable audit log for all request lifecycle events and AI actions (generation, edits, suggestions accepted).
  • Availability and performance: Define uptime target; typical page loads under agreed threshold; handle peak submission periods.
  • Accessibility: WCAG-aligned UI; keyboard navigation; screen reader support.
  • Localization/timezone handling: Store timestamps in UTC, display in user timezone; support local policy differences.
  • Data retention: Configurable retention per region and policy; support legal holds.
  • AI governance: Model output monitoring, prompt logging, bias testing, safe completion rules, and human override.

10. Reporting & analytics

Standard dashboards

  • Requests volume by type, region, department.
  • Cycle time and SLA compliance by approver group.
  • Decision outcomes by type and time.
  • Outcome effectiveness trends (ratings, reversion rate, follow-up issues).
  • Reconsideration rate and reasons.

Metric definitions

  • Approval rate: approvals / submitted requests.
  • SLA compliance: percent of approval steps decided within SLA.
  • Rework rate: percent of submissions returned for more info.
  • Consistency index: variance of approval rates across teams normalized for request type and role level.

Filters and breakdowns

  • Department, team, manager, job level, location, region, request type, urgency, duration, policy tag.
  • Protected attributes: only in aggregated form and only when permitted.

Fairness monitoring considerations

  • Avoid individual-level exposure of sensitive data.
  • Use statistical thresholds for flagging anomalies.
  • Provide drill-down only to authorized roles and only when compliant.

11. Edge cases & exception handling

  • Missing or inactive approver: auto-delegate or route to HR ops queue.
  • Conflicting requests: detect overlap with existing flexible arrangements; prompt for clarification.
  • Policy mismatch: request violates policy constraints; require exception workflow.
  • Withdrawal after submission: allow withdrawal with reason; log and notify approvers.
  • Appeal/reconsideration: enforce time window and required new information.
  • Long-running arrangements: require scheduled re-approval or check-in cadence.
  • Employee transfer or manager change mid-process: re-evaluate routing and notify stakeholders.
  • AI hallucinated policy references: system must only suggest from verified policy library or clearly mark placeholders.
